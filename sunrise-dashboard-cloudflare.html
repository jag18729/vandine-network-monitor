The Homelab That Started It All

Hey there! I'm Rafael, and this is my network operations center... running from my mom's house. Yeah, you read that right. While most people store old furniture in their parents' basement, I've turned it into a full-scale enterprise network lab.

It started innocently enough - "Mom, can I just put this little Raspberry Pi here to block ads?" Fast forward two years, and now there's a Palo Alto PA-220 firewall humming away next to her holiday decorations, a UniFi Dream Machine managing VLANs for the whole house, and a cluster of Raspberry Pis orchestrating containers like it's a Silicon Valley data center.

The best part? Mom gets ad-free browsing and I get to tell recruiters I manage a "geographically distributed edge computing infrastructure with zero-trust architecture." We both win! Plus, her internet is probably more secure than most Fortune 500 companies at this point.

Current achievement: 99.97% uptime (the 0.03% was when Mom accidentally unplugged it to vacuum)
